How does the multi-vector retriever handle skewed document distributions in the alpha tenant?

bolt
The multi-vector retriever in the Enterprise-Alpha tenant addresses skewed document distributions by employing a dynamic weight rebalancing algorithm during the retrieval phase. It creates parent-child relationships where large documents are partitioned into semantically cohesive chunks (child vectors) while maintaining the global context of the parent document. When a skew is detected—specifically in the distribution of vector density across metadata namespaces—the system applies a TF-IDF inspired normalization to prevent high-density clusters from drowning out sparse but relevant data points.
